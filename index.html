Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

Honoring the Father of the Nation

Mahatma Gandhi, often called the Father of the Nation, was a leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. Known for his philosophy of nonviolence (Ahimsa) and truth (Satyagraha), he inspired countless movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Achievements & Contributions

Nonviolent Resistance

He led the Indian independence movement through nonviolence, inspiring global leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.

Salt March

The Salt March of 1930 demonstrated his ability to unite the nation against injustice and gained international attention.

Social Reform

He worked tirelessly to eradicate untouchability, promote women's rights, and improve the lives of the poor in India.

Legacy

Mahatma Gandhi's teachings of peace, nonviolence, and truth continue to inspire millions. His legacy remains a guiding light for humanity, proving that lasting change can be achieved through peaceful means. The world celebrates his life and contributions every year on October 2, observed as the International Day of Nonviolence.
